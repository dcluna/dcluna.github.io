History

There were over 500 different clan groups or 'nations' around the continent, many with distinctive cultures, beliefs and languages.

Today, Indigenous people make up 2.4 per cent of the total Australian population (about 460,000 out of 24 million people).

Australian colonization

In 1788, two years after the decision to colonise Australia was made, Captain Arthur Phillip and 1,500 convicts, crew, marines and civilians arrived at Sydney Cove.

The colonisation of Australia had a devastating impact on the Indigenous people who had lived on this land for over 60,000 years.

Children of Australian Aboriginal descent are removed from their families by Australian and State government agencies. This practice lasted 100 years and is known as the Stolen Generation.

Nowadays Australia is a modern, multicultural country

Flora and fauna

Most plants in Australia are well adapted to the arid (dry) conditions of the continent. The main vegetation type in Australia is the hummock grasslands. These grasslands can be found in Western Australia, South Australia and the Northern Territory. Grassland is a grass-dominated area where few or no trees grow.
